Our Legacy

Three generations.
One unchanged recipe book.

It began in 1926, when Sulemanbhai — a young bakery assistant from Poona — opened a tiny sweet shop outside a mosque in Bombay. His son Abdul Gafoor Suleman Usman Mithaiwala guarded the family formulas through partition, prosperity and change.

Today the third generation carries that legacy across India and the GCC — twelve branches strong, but the same brass karahis, the same slow-cooked mawa, the same hands-on technique that made the name a household word.

Signature Specialities

The ones we’re famous for.

A handful of dishes have travelled with the brand for nearly a century. These are the must-try.

Pista Aflatoon

The legendary house signature — slow-cooked semolina, mawa, ghee, saffron and pistachio. Cut warm. Served with pride.

House icon · since 1926

Mawa Jalebi

Crisp on the outside, dense and milky inside — soaked in fragrant cardamom-saffron syrup. Best within the hour.

Made fresh through the day

Malpua & Rabdi

Pillowy fried discs in chilled, reduced rabdi — a Bombay-style ritual every Eid, Diwali and feast night.

House classic

Dry Fruit Halwa

Walnut, fig and almond, simmered low with pure ghee until glossy. A winter favourite, available year-round.

Akhrot · Anjeer · Gajar · Dudhi

Seekh & Tikka

Char-grilled chicken malai tikka, mutton seekh kabab and paneer tikka — straight off the smouldering tandoor.

Tandoor specialities

Mutton Khichra

The eight-hour Bohra-style khichra — cracked wheat, lentils and slow-cooked mutton, finished with fried onions.

Weekend speciality
