Our Heritage

Where every sweet carries a story

It began with Sulemanbhai — a young bakery assistant from Poona, who arrived in Bombay with a single ambition: to make sweets so honest and so generous that strangers would return as family. In 1936, he set up a modest shop just outside Minara Masjid on Mohammed Ali Road.

Nine decades later, that quiet little stall has grown into one of India's most iconic mithai houses. Through generations — from Sulemanbhai to his son Abdul Gafoor, and the family that carries the name today — the recipes, the standards, and the warmth have remained untouched by time.
