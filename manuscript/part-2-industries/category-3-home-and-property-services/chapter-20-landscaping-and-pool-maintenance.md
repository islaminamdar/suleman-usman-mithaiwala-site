PART TWO — THE FIELD GUIDE  ·  HOME & PROPERTY SERVICES

# Chapter 20 · Landscaping & Pool Maintenance

*A recurring-revenue, labour-heavy business serving a city that insists on greenery and water despite a climate that makes both expensive to maintain.*

---

## 1 · What this business actually is in Dubai

Landscaping and pool maintenance in Dubai encompasses two related but operationally distinct services, frequently offered by the same company.

**Landscaping** covers the design, installation, and ongoing maintenance of outdoor spaces: lawns, planted beds, irrigation systems, trees, ground cover, boundary hedging, and hard landscaping elements such as paving, water features, and outdoor lighting. In Dubai, installation projects — a new garden for a villa or a planted entrance for a residential community — provide lump-sum project revenue. Maintenance contracts — the monthly or weekly servicing of established outdoor spaces — provide the recurring revenue that underpins the business.

**Pool maintenance** covers the chemical management, mechanical servicing, and cleaning of swimming pools. In Dubai, a large proportion of villas, residential compounds, and serviced apartment buildings have private or shared pools. Pools in Dubai's climate require chemical balancing at a frequency that most owners cannot or will not manage themselves, and the filtration and pump systems require regular inspection and periodic repair. A monthly pool maintenance contract is, for most pool owners, not a luxury — it is a functional necessity.

Both services share the same operating model: recurring maintenance contracts, delivered by a field workforce that moves between sites on a fixed schedule, generating predictable monthly billing and building retention-based revenue. The key variables are how efficiently the workforce is routed between sites, how reliably it delivers the agreed scope, and how well the chemistry and plant management is controlled.

Dubai Municipality has oversight of pool water quality standards and, in the landscaping context, of pesticide and chemical use. Operating within those standards is both a regulatory requirement and a selling point with community clients who manage shared spaces.

## 2 · Capital required

**Licence and approvals:** A DET commercial licence covering landscaping and pool maintenance activities is the starting point. Budget AED 12,000–20,000 for the licence and associated fees in the first year, though these figures are subject to change and should be confirmed with DET directly.

**Staff and visas** are the dominant cost, as in all labour-intensive property services. A minimum viable team for a combined landscaping and pool business might be three labourers or technicians and a supervisor. First-year visa and establishment costs per employee run approximately AED 15,000–25,000, covering the visa itself, Emirates ID, medical tests, and MOHRE registration. If you recruit overseas, add agency and travel costs.

**Accommodation** is a practical and legal requirement for many of the workers you will employ. Factor this in from the outset — shared accommodation for three to five workers can add AED 20,000–40,000 to your annual cost base, depending on location.

**Equipment and materials:** A landscaping business requires mowers, edge trimmers, pruning equipment, irrigation tools, and a supply of plants and soil amendments. A pool maintenance team requires chemical testing kits, water treatment chemicals, brushes, vacuums, and the tools for basic pump and filter servicing. A starter equipment kit for both services costs AED 25,000–50,000.

**Transport:** Landscaping equipment does not travel in a car. A suitable pick-up truck or small van costs AED 35,000–60,000 purchased outright, or AED 2,500–4,000 per month leased.

**Working capital** must cover wages, accommodation, vehicle costs, and chemical supplies through the early months before contract revenue is consistent.

A lean start serving residential villas: AED 140,000–210,000. A properly capitalised operation targeting community contracts: AED 280,000–420,000. Verify all figures before finalising your plan.

## 3 · Licence & jurisdiction

**DET commercial licence** covering landscaping or gardening services and swimming pool maintenance is the standard route. Both activities can typically be listed on a single commercial licence, though confirm the exact activity codes with DET before filing, as the classification can affect what you are permitted to do.

**Dubai Municipality** has specific relevance in two areas. First, the application of pesticides and herbicides in Dubai is subject to DM regulation — operators using controlled chemicals in outdoor spaces may be required to use only registered products and, in some contexts, to hold a pest control licence or employ certified applicators. Confirm the current requirements with DM before you begin any chemical application work. Second, pool water quality falls under DM standards, particularly for pools in residential communities, hotels, and facilities accessible to the public. Knowing and adhering to these standards is a basic requirement.

**MOHRE** governs all employment relationships on the mainland. Ensure your payroll runs through the Wage Protection System, and keep employment contracts up to date.

**Free zone vs mainland:** A free zone entity is not the appropriate structure for a business physically delivering services across Dubai's residential communities and villa compounds. A mainland DET licence is the correct choice.

**Practical recommendation:** Register both activities on your licence from the start. Landscaping installation projects and maintenance contracts often come together, and being licensed for both avoids the need to return to DET for an activity amendment later.

## 4 · First three customers

**Approach villa community managers directly.** Residential villa communities — whether managed by a developer's FM arm or an independent property management company — maintain landscaped common areas and often communal pools. The community manager is the single decision-maker for these contracts. An introduction — through a shared contact, a direct visit to the management office, or a letter of introduction with your licence documentation and a brief capability statement — can produce a contract covering an entire community's outdoor spaces. A single community contract may be worth AED 80,000–200,000 per year in recurring maintenance revenue, depending on its size.

**Target individual villa owners through referral.** The villa owner market in established Dubai communities is dense and well-connected. A homeowners' association WhatsApp group, a community newsletter, or a recommendation from a real estate agent who manages villa lettings can put your name in front of dozens of potential pool and garden maintenance clients at once. Offer an initial assessment visit at no charge: the ability to walk the garden and pool with an owner and present a clear, professional scope and pricing document builds confidence faster than any advertisement.

**Work through pool equipment suppliers and landscaping nurseries.** Pool equipment retailers and landscaping nurseries interact with villa owners and property managers who are already experiencing problems — a broken pump, a pool turning green, a garden that has outgrown its current maintenance regime. An arrangement with two or three suppliers to refer you to customers with immediate needs is a cost-effective lead source and a natural fit: the supplier solves the equipment problem, you solve the ongoing maintenance problem.

## 5 · Profit margins & cash cycle

Recurring maintenance contracts — both landscaping and pool — are the financial engine of this business. A monthly pool maintenance contract for a standard residential pool might be priced at AED 500–1,000 per month, depending on size and scope, at the time of writing; confirm current market rates, as they vary. A monthly garden maintenance contract for a mid-sized villa plot might run AED 600–1,500 per month. Neither number is large individually, but the model is built on volume and retention: fifty pool contracts at AED 750 per month is AED 37,500 per month in recurring revenue, and the marginal cost of adding a contract to an existing route is low.

Gross margins on maintenance contracts typically run 40–55%. The constraints are the labour cost of the technicians delivering the service and the chemical cost for pool maintenance, which is real and fluctuates with import prices.

Installation and landscaping project work carries lower gross margins — typically 25–40% — because materials are a significant cost component and project pricing must account for contingencies.

Net margin for a well-run operation with a strong contract portfolio sits at 15–22%. The primary margin risk is routing inefficiency: a team spending excessive travel time between sites, or servicing contracts that are geographically spread, loses productive capacity that cannot be recovered.

**The cash cycle** is relatively favourable compared to other property services categories, because residential clients typically pay monthly maintenance fees on time or in advance. B2B community contracts may carry 30–45 day terms, which is manageable. The seasonal dynamic is the more significant cash consideration: the Dubai summer (June through August) suppresses landscaping installation project demand, as neither clients nor labourers relish outdoor work in 45-degree heat. Revenue from maintenance contracts holds through the summer; new project revenue does not. Plan your cash accordingly.

## 6 · Top 3 mistakes

**Underpricing maintenance contracts to win volume, then finding the margin cannot cover the operating cost.** The temptation for a new entrant is to price below established operators to win their first community or villa contracts. The problem is that maintenance contracts are renewed annually, and the price you set in year one becomes the baseline for year two. If that price does not cover your labour, accommodation, transport, and chemical costs, you are not building a business — you are subsidising your clients' gardens. Price your contracts to cover your full cost of delivery from the first invoice.

**Ignoring the seasonal profile in your cash planning.** Landscaping installation projects dry up in the summer months, and while maintenance contracts continue, the revenue mix shifts significantly. Founders who plan their annual cash position on a flat monthly projection find themselves short in July and August when installation revenues are minimal. Model your cash by month, not by year, and ensure your runway covers the summer dip in your first two years.

**Failing to maintain chemical compliance documentation for pool work.** Pool maintenance in a residential community or a shared facility involves chemicals — chlorine, pH adjustors, algaecides — that Dubai Municipality regulates. Using unregistered products, applying chemicals without trained staff, or being unable to produce water quality test records when a municipality inspector or a community manager asks for them creates immediate problems. Build your chemical compliance record-keeping into the weekly routine of every pool technician from the first contract.

## 7 · Who's already winning

The operators winning in this category today tend to hold one of two positions. The first is the large integrated FM company that includes landscaping and pool maintenance as components of a wider property services bundle. These firms win by bundling: a developer or community manager who can place a single contract covering cleaning, maintenance, landscaping, and pool management is an attractive client, and the FM company that can deliver all four holds a structural advantage over smaller specialists.

The second winning position is the focused specialist: a landscaping company known for a particular style of garden design, or a pool maintenance company with a documented quality process and consistent technicians. These operators win residential and boutique commercial clients who value expertise over price, and they grow almost entirely through referral. The key differentiator is consistency: the same technician on the same schedule, the same chemical balance achieved every visit, and the proactive communication when something is wrong rather than waiting for the client to notice.

The operators struggling are those who have too many clients spread over too wide a geography, who lose technicians to competitors and cannot maintain service quality, and who have not built the routing efficiency that makes the economics work. The operational model of this business rewards discipline and density over reach.

---

## 8 · Verdict

Landscaping and pool maintenance is one of the most genuinely recurring businesses in the Home and Property Services category. The contracts renew, the work does not change, and the demand is structural — as long as Dubai continues to build villas with gardens and pools, this business has customers.

The challenge is not market demand; it is operational discipline. The business only makes money if the routing is tight, the labour is reliable, the chemicals are right, and the clients stay. Each of those requirements involves systems — scheduling systems, staff management systems, chemical documentation systems — that a founder has to build before they become necessary, not after.

This suits the patient operator who is comfortable with a field workforce, who understands that margins are made in the logistics rather than the sales, and who is prepared to grow the contract portfolio steadily rather than quickly.

It does not suit the founder who is expecting fast revenue, who underestimates the summer cash dip, or who has not planned for the accommodation and transport costs that underpin every technician in the team.

**Bottom line:** A properly structured landscaping and pool maintenance business in Dubai can generate reliable, growing recurring revenue — but only if the operator invests in the systems and the staff from day one and resists the temptation to undercut on price in the pursuit of volume.
