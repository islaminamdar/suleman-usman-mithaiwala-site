PART THREE — RESOURCES

# Glossary of Dubai business terms

*The vocabulary of setting up — defined plainly, in one place.*

---

A founder meets a great deal of new vocabulary in the first weeks, much of it abbreviated, some of it used loosely even by people who should know better. This glossary defines the terms as they are used in this book and in practice. Where a term names an authority or a fee, treat the surrounding detail as indicative and subject to change, and confirm the current position through the official channel.

**Audit.** An independent examination of a company's financial statements. Required for some activities and by many free zones; confirm whether your jurisdiction and activity require one.

**Commercial licence.** A licence for trading activities — buying and selling goods. One of the main licence categories alongside professional and industrial.

**Corporate tax.** The UAE federal tax on business profits: 9% on profits above AED 375,000, and 0% below that threshold, for financial years starting on or after 1 June 2023. Administered by the FTA.

**Customs code.** A registration with customs that allows a company to import and export goods. Needed by trading and logistics businesses; obtained after the licence is issued.

**DET — Dubai Department of Economy and Tourism.** The authority that licenses mainland companies in Dubai, formerly known as the DED. Governs mainland trade names, activities, initial approvals, and licence renewals.

**DLD — Dubai Land Department.** The authority regulating real estate in Dubai. RERA operates within it.

**Ejari.** The system that registers a commercial or residential tenancy contract in Dubai. An Ejari-registered lease is required to use real premises as a licensed address.

**Establishment card.** The document, issued after the trade licence, that authorises a company to sponsor residence visas. Also called the immigration card. Nothing on the visa side can begin without it.

**Flexi-desk.** A shared or hot-desk package offered by many free zones that satisfies the requirement for a registered address without a private office. Common for lean service businesses and a low-cost entry point.

**Free zone.** A designated economic area with its own licensing authority, activity list, and rules. Dubai has 40-plus free zones. They permit 100% foreign ownership and suit businesses serving international or business clients.

**Free zone authority.** The body that governs a particular free zone — for example DMCC, IFZA, Meydan, or Dubai South. It issues licences, registers names, provides premises, and usually processes visas for companies in its zone.

**FTA — Federal Tax Authority.** The federal body administering VAT and corporate tax: registration, returns, and deregistration.

**GDRFA — General Directorate of Residency and Foreigners Affairs.** The authority governing entry permits, residence visas, and the residency status of those a company sponsors.

**Golden Visa.** A long-term UAE residence visa, typically valid for five or ten years, granted to qualifying investors, entrepreneurs, skilled professionals, and others. Eligibility criteria change; confirm the current rules.

**Immigration card.** Another name for the establishment card — the document that lets a company sponsor visas.

**Industrial licence.** A licence for manufacturing and industrial activities — processing, assembly, or production of goods.

**Initial approval.** An authority's confirmation, in principle, that it has no objection to a proposed activity and the proposed owners. A milestone early in setup; it is not the licence itself.

**KHDA — Knowledge and Human Development Authority.** The authority governing private education and training in Dubai — schools, nurseries, and training and tutoring centres.

**Mainland.** A company licensed by DET that may trade directly anywhere in the UAE, including to the public and government. 100% foreign ownership is permitted for the large majority of activities since the 2021 reform.

**MoA — Memorandum of Association.** The constitutional document of a company, setting out its shareholders, share structure, and governance. Free zones use this or an equivalent document.

**MOHRE — Ministry of Human Resources and Emiratisation.** The federal ministry governing labour and employment for mainland companies — contracts, work permits, the WPS, and Emiratisation.

**NOC — No-Objection Certificate.** A formal letter from a relevant party — an employer, a sponsor, or an authority — stating it has no objection to a proposed action, such as a person setting up or joining a company.

**Offshore.** A company used purely for holding, intellectual property, and international structuring — for example JAFZA Offshore or RAK ICC. It has no UAE operating presence and grants no residence visas.

**PRO — Public Relations Officer.** A person, often engaged through a service firm, who handles government paperwork — licensing, visas, and approvals — on a company's behalf. A common way for founders to navigate procedures efficiently.

**Professional licence.** A licence for service-based activities that rely on intellectual or professional skill — consultancy, marketing, IT services, and similar.

**Qualifying free zone person.** A free zone company that, by meeting specific conditions, may retain a 0% corporate tax rate on its qualifying income. The conditions are detailed and have evolved; confirm the current rules with the FTA.

**RERA — Real Estate Regulatory Agency.** The agency within the DLD that regulates the real estate profession — brokerage, broker permits and examinations, and property management.

**RTA — Roads and Transport Authority.** The Dubai authority governing vehicles, transport, and mobility permits — relevant to car rental, chauffeur services, and delivery operations. Salik sits under its wider remit.

**Salik.** Dubai's automated road-toll system. A charge is deducted electronically each time a vehicle passes a toll gate — a running cost for any vehicle-based business.

**Tasheel.** The MOHRE service channel through which much mainland labour and employment paperwork — contracts and work permits — is processed.

**Tourism Dirham.** A levy applied to guest stays in hotels and holiday homes, collected per room per night. Relevant to hospitality and holiday-homes businesses.

**Trade name.** The registered commercial name of a company. It must meet the authority's naming rules and is reserved early in setup, before or alongside initial approval.

**Trakheesi.** DET's system for processing many mainland permits, approvals, and licensing-related transactions in Dubai.

**VAT — Value Added Tax.** A consumption tax of 5%, in force in the UAE since 2018. Registration with the FTA is mandatory once taxable supplies meet the threshold, and optional below it. Confirm the current thresholds with the FTA.

**Working capital.** The cash that funds day-to-day operations — stock, salaries, rent, marketing — before a business reliably pays for itself. Not a setup fee; the fuel that carries the business through its runway.

**WPS — Wage Protection System.** The UAE system through which employers pay salaries electronically so that wages are paid in full and on time. Required for mainland employers.
